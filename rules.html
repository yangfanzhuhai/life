Rules of Life

You have to learn the rules of the game.
And then you have to play better than anyone else.

Albert Einstein

zero-player

Alter the initial configuration and observe how it unfolds.
The game's evolution is determined by its initial state, requiring no further input.

edgeless grid

The universe of the game is set out as a grid of cells. The grid is often assumed to be infinite.
In this implementation the grid is wrapped around as a torus (or dough-nut) so that each cell has eight neighbouring cells.

life or death

Each cell is in one of two possible states: alive or dead.
At each step in time, the following transitions occur:

  1. Any live cell with fewer than two live neighbours dies, as if by isolation.
  2. Any live cell with two or three live neighbours lives on to the next generation.
  3. Any live cell with more than three live neighbours dies, as if by overcrowding.
  4. Any dead cell with exactly three live neighbours becomes a live cell, as if by reproduction.

colourful life

Cells also have a colour: either red or green.
When a new cell is born it inherits the majority colour of its surrounding cells.
